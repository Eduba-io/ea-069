01 / The Eduba frame

Dual-use scales only when the governance layer is designed on purpose.

We work on a simple idea. About 60% of what an ML-core product team does is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a genuine AI problem. The job is sorting those three into the right layers before anything ships, and documenting the sort so a federal reviewer and a state insurance regulator can both follow it.

Applied to the Clearspeed stack, the map resolves quickly. Audio ingestion, feature extraction, model hosting, and telemetry logging are code-and-database work. Decision thresholds, fairness testing, and audit-trail requirements are rule-based logic. The voice-risk scoring model itself is the genuinely AI layer. The work of writing down which federal evidence and which insurance-regulator evidence comes out of which layer is the compounding work.

That is the work we would propose to do with the product and governance team before the next carrier integration and the next federal ATO cycle.

60 / 30 / 10 as it maps to Clearspeed
  1. 60%Database & code
    Audio ingestion. Feature extraction. Model hosting. Telemetry and audit log.
  2. 30%Rule-based logic
    Decision thresholds. Fairness gates. RMF and state-regulator evidence capture.
  3. 10%Genuine AI
    Voice-risk scoring model. Drift detection. Language-independent feature learning.
